Our History

32 Years of Empowering South Florida’s Business Community

The South Florida Hispanic Chamber of Commerce (SFLHCC) is a nonprofit 501(c)(6) organization established in 1994 to promote business leadership, create economic prospects, and provide legislative advocacy for the business community in South Florida.

The SFLHCC is recognized as one of the most influential Hispanic chambers in the country and one of the leading Hispanic business organizations in Florida.

The SFLHCC is an organization founded 32 years ago, and it seeks to provide opportunities in commerce to the different populations that exist in South Florida. As South Florida is one of the most diverse areas of the state, the SFLHCC recognizes the potential that exists for companies to do business with people from different backgrounds and experiences. With a population of approximately 67% Hispanics, Miami-Dade County has flourished as the gateway to the Americas. This community is represented by entrepreneurs of the U.S., Caribbean, Central America, and South America.

We provide opportunities for our members by organizing local programs to promote and elevate Hispanic-owned and non-Hispanic-owned businesses. The SFLHCC is an association with more than 1,600 members and advocates for thousands of Hispanic and non-Hispanic businesses in Florida.

{# ===================== MISSION STATEMENT ===================== #}

Mission Statement

Advancing the Commercial, Financial, Educational & Civic Interests of South Florida

The South Florida Hispanic Chamber of Commerce (SFLHCC) was founded on July 21, 1994, with the mission of promoting the continued growth and development of the Hispanic and non-Hispanic business community and to serve as a resource center and forum to advocate for businesses. The organization is comprised of dedicated professional individuals and business owners who have come together to advance the commercial, financial, educational, and civic interests of the ever-growing community of South Florida.
